Barri Gòtic
Barcelona, the capital of the Catalan Region in Spain, is frequented by thousands of tourists every year with its unique natural beauties, historical artifacts and architecture.

Barri Gòtic, the most mystical area of the city with its exciting streets, is one of the favorite destinations of travelers. Therefore, in our guide where to stay in Barcelona, the first place should be reserved for this region.

If you like fun, the Barri Gòtic area is for you. The streets of Barcelona's oldest and liveliest neighborhood are lined with Roman and medieval buildings, as well as numerous modern cafes, restaurants, bars, clubs and shops. The Gothic Quarter, which is part of the area, is also home to the Barcelona Cathedral, built between the 13th and 15th centuries.

The famous street La Rambla is located in the center of the Barri Gotic district. Here you can also see the world-famous La Boqueria Market, where authentic food and various spices are sold.

Barri Gòtic, where the historical heart of the city beats, has a fascinating atmosphere with its narrow and labyrinth-like streets. You can find all kinds of clothing and souvenirs, from local shops to world-famous brands, in the region, whose streets are always lively and colorful.

Suitable for those who want to stay close to the center, Barri Gòtic is an ideal choice especially for those who love nightlife. Although the hotels in the area are a little more expensive than the standard ones, it is worth it to step outside the hotel and be in the heart of the city, wander the windy streets and shop at the endless number of shops.

Sagrada Familia
Sagrada Familia Basilica, one of the symbols of Barcelona and also on the UNESCO List, is shown as the greatest work of the famous architect Antoni Gaudi, who contributed to the popularity of the city.

In fact, for the construction of the basilica, the Religious Union of San Jose first agreed with Francisco de Paula de Villar y Lozano. However, when disagreements emerged between the two sides, the project was handed over to Gaudi in 1883 and he devoted his life to the building until 1926, when he died as a result of an accident.

Although the basilica was left unfinished after the death of the great master, it is considered as one of the must-see structures in Barcelona today.

If you also spare time for this flamboyant structure, you can take the sections of the Great Passion Gate, the Nativity Facade, the Burial Chamber, the Bell Towers, and the Spiral Stairs to the focal point of your tour.

Located in the northern part of Barcelona, with a capacity of 13 thousand people, the Sagrada Familia dominates the entire region with its 18 slender towers rising above all other monuments in the city. The work, which has been under construction for almost 140 years, is open to the public, although it is still under construction.

Visiting Information:

Entrance fees can vary between €20 – €33 depending on the points you will visit in the building (towers etc) and guided/unguided tours.

Sagrada Familia Basilica visiting hours vary according to the seasons. While the opening time is usually 09:00, the closing time can vary between 18.00 and 20.00 depending on the season.

Carrer de Mallorca, 401, 08013 Barcelona, Spain


Park Güell
Park Güell, another Antoni Gaudi work; It has a surreal beauty with its colorful, cheerful appearance and interesting architecture. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the park was built between 1900 and 1914 and, like the Sagrada Familia, reflects Gaudi's architectural originality.

In the park, caves resembling a playground, entertaining structures resembling a water park, bridges and structures covered with colorful ceramics reflecting the dynamism of the park and winding stairs fascinate visitors.

While the magnificent terrace reveals the panoramic view of the city and the sea, there is also the house where Gaudi lived in the park, and today it serves as the Gaudi Museum, where Gaudi's works are exhibited. After visiting the museum, you can also spend time in the green park of the museum and take a pleasant break from your trip.

Eusebi Güell, one of the wealthiest industrialists in Barcelona, asked the famous architect Gaudi to build a site with 60 residences reflecting Catalan modernism, but this project was unfinished. A fascinating place with its figures reflecting nature and vibrant colors, Park Güell was originally designed as a revolutionary housing estate, but since no investment was made in the project, it was donated to the local council and turned into a public park in 1922.

Visiting Information:

Some parts of the park are free, but if you want to visit the entire park, you have to pay an entrance fee of €10.

You can visit the park, which is open every day of the year, between 08.30 and 2.30.

08024, Barcelona, Spaim


Barcelona FC Museum
Another iconic place that you can add to your list of places to visit in Barcelona is the magnificent Camp Nou Stadium, where the Barcelona Football Club, known by the locals as the unofficial national team of the Autonomous Region of Catalonia, plays its matches, and the Barcelona FC Museum.

The stadium, designed by Catalan Architect Francesc Mitjans and built on an area of 55,000 square meters in 3 years, has a capacity of close to 99 thousand.

In the museum, the history of the football club over 100 years is conveyed to the visitors with visual and auditory presentations. In the cultural venue where you can see all the trophies that Barça has won, works donated by local artists such as Dali, Miro, Subirach and Taples are exhibited.

The stadium, which is 48 meters high, 250 meters long and 220 meters wide, can be evacuated in just 5 minutes. During the stadium tour, you can visit areas such as the tunnel, locker rooms, showers and press tribune used by the players to go to the field.

Visiting Information:

Standard ticket is €26, Camp Nou Tour Plus is €35. If you want to buy your ticket at the box office, you have to pay an additional €3.5.

Stadium and museum visiting hours may change from time to time. Usually from January to April and on Sundays from 10:00 to 14:30; you can visit between 09.30 and 19.30 in other months and days.

Carrer de Aritides Maillol, 12, 08028 Barcelona, Spain


Picasso Museum
The Picasso Museum, which is the first museum opened in his name while the Spanish artist was alive, has more than 3,500 works in its collection.

The cultural facility, which opened its doors to visitors in 1963, thus shares the title of the museum that houses the most works of Picasso with the museum in Paris.

Operating within the Palacio de Berenguer d'Aguliar, which was used as a mansion in the Middle Ages, where Picasso spent a period of his life, the first period works of the artist are exhibited rather than his well-known works.

Hombre Con Boina, Autorretrato con Peluca, Ciencia y Caridad, Menu de Els Quatro Gats are some of the works you can see when you visit the museum.

Visiting Information:

Entrance fees €12.

You can visit the museum between 10.00 -17.00 on Monday, 10.00 - 21.30 on Thursday, 09.00 - 20.30 on Tuesday and Sunday. The museum is closed on public holidays.

Carrer Montcada, 15-23, 08003, Barcelona, Spain


Parc de la Ciutadella
Parc de La Ciutadella, whose part close to the sea was reserved for the Barcelona Zoo, which was established on an area of ​​14 hectares in 1892, was arranged by King Felipe V in 1888 instead of a castle built for the safety of the city after the War of Spanish Succession in 1714.

The park, which includes the building of the Catalan Parliament, is a place frequented by the people of the city and tourists thanks to its walking paths and green areas.

When you go to the park, you can first visit the Font Monumental, which Gaudi contributed while he was a student, and the Natural History and Geology museums, who produced many works for the city. Then you can head to the Arc de Triomphe at the exit of Passeig Lluis Company Street.

Parc de la Ciutadella, which stands out with its features that embrace the history of Barcelona, proves this feature with its Hivernacle Winter Garden and Umbracle greenhouse. You should definitely visit these historical gardens where beautiful plants and flowers are exhibited.

Visiting Information:

Entry to the park is free, but there is an entrance fee of €18 for the Zoo within the park and €4 for the National Science museum.

The park is open all year round from 10:00 to 22:30.

Passeig de Picasso, 21, 08003, Barcelona, Spain


Barcelona Cathedral
Barcelona Cathedral (Cathedral of the Holy Cross and Saint Eulalia) is a magnificent Gothic church located in Barcelona's charming Gothic Quarter.

Although overlooked due to the famous Sagrada Familia dominating the city, the Barcelona Cathedral is one of the largest and most impressive religious buildings in Spain.

Founded on the site of an old chapel in the 15th century, the cathedral is dedicated to Barcelona Eulalia, the common patron saint of the city, buried in the cellar of the building. You can see beautiful examples of Gothic architecture and Roman walls as you walk around the cathedral, which has a stunning roof and majestic bell towers rising to the sky.

The most beautiful part of the cathedral is probably the charming 14th-century cloister courtyard, recognized by many as "the most beautiful oasis in Barcelona". Another highlight of the Barcelona Cathedral is the elevator that takes visitors to the roof and offers them magnificent views of the Gothic Quarter and Barcelona.

In front of the Cathedral, which has an attraction and beauty that should be on the list of places to visit in Barcelona, there is a small square where you will find street performers, cafes and interesting shops.

Barcelona Cathedral harbors an ancient legend. Legend has it that the Romans stripped St. Eulalia naked because of her religious beliefs and left her to die in the middle of the street. Although the event takes place in the spring season, it suddenly snows and the snow completely covers St. Eulalia, protecting it. However, this angered the Romans. To kill him, they put him in a barrel covered with a knife, and St. Eulalia dies.

Visiting Information:

You have to pay €7 for standard tickets and €4 more for tickets with audio guide.

The cathedral, which is open every day of the year; It can be visited between 12.00 – 17.45 on weekdays, 12.30 – 17.45 on Saturdays and 14.00-17.30 on Sundays.

Pla de la Seu, s/n, 08002 Barcelona, Spain


Tibidabo Hill
The 512-meter-high Tibidabo Hill, where the magnificent Church of the Sacred Heart, whose construction began in 1902 and was completed in 60 years by blending Modernist-Neo Gothic styles, is located, is the highest part of the Collserola Mountains.

On the hill, which offers its guests an impressive view of the city thanks to its location, apart from the church, there is also the 288-meter-tall communication tower, which you can reach by elevator, and the Parc d'Atraccions del Tibidabo, which was opened in 1908 and is the only amusement park in the city where old-fashioned toys are working.

You can use the funicular to reach the hill, which will allow you to have pleasant hours during your Barcelona holiday, or you can take the Tibi Bus departing from Catalonia Square.

You can even reach the 3 places I mentioned by walking on the path where you can take a break when necessary.

In Latin, "Tibi babo" means "I offer you". According to legend, the devil He took Jesus to the top of the hill and showed the magnificent city view and said, “I present to you”. The name of the hill is based on this rumor.

Visiting Information:

Admission to Tibidabo Hill and the Church of the Sacred Heart of Jesus is free. However, you have to pay €15 for the part where the statue of Jesus is located. also for the amusement park it is €28.5 for adults and €10 for children.

Tibidabo hill is closed to visitors in the evening. You can visit the Church of the Sacred Heart of Jesus, located on the hill, between 11:00 and 21:00 every day of the year.

Tibidabo / 08035 Barcelona / Spain


Basilica of Santa Maria del Mar
Shown as one of the most successful examples of Gothic churches due to its architectural harmony, the Basilica of Santa Maria del Mar was built between 1329 and 1384 with the support of the residents of La Ribera.

The simplicity of the exterior of the basilica, which bears the title of being the only structure in the city with a completely Catalan Gothic style, leaves its place to the rich details masterfully processed inside.

While the 15th century ship on the altar of the religious building, which Gaudi took as an example when designing La Sagrada Familia, reminds the years when the city was at its peak in maritime, the most important part of the basilica is the monument called Fossar de les Moreres, where the soldiers who died in the war in 1714 are buried.

The Basilica of Santa Maria del Mar is dedicated to the patron saint of sailors. Inside the basilica, there is a ship dating from the 15th century and this ship symbolizes the success of the Spanish in maritime.

Visiting Information:

Full ticket is €10, student €8.5.

You can visit the museum, which is open every day of the year, between 13.00 - 17.00 on weekdays and Saturdays, and between 14.00 - 17.00 on Sundays.

Plaça de Santa Maria, 08003, Barcelona, Spain

Foods to be Tasted


Paella
First on the list is Paella, originally from Valencia. The name of the dish means "Frying" in our language.

The main ingredient of Paella, one of the most well-known and sought-after flavors of Spanish food culture, is short rice. The fact that it offers the opportunity to experience different flavors at the same time makes the meal quite special.

Paella is prepared using a variety of seafood. However, in some restaurants, this palatable recipe; Chicken, beef and game meat or forms with different ingredients such as sausage and sausage can be offered. You can even add wine to enhance its flavor.

Therefore, if you pay attention to the consumption of halal products, it may be to your advantage to learn about the content before ordering the food.

To taste this flavor in Barcelona, you can choose L’Arrosseria Xàtiva. Adresi: Carrer del Torrent d’En Vidalet, 26, 08012


Escalivada
Escalivada, a true Catalonian flavor, is perfect for those who like sooty flavors. Moreover, due to its content, it is ideal for individuals with vegan and vegetarian eating habits.

The name of the dish means "Frying on embers" in our language.

One serving of Escalivada contains roasted eggplant, seasoned with olive oil in the most classic way, bell peppers, onions, tomatoes, garlic and salt.

Catalans like to consume this dish either as the start of a big feast or alongside meat dishes. So you can act in a similar way. Most restaurants serve it in the form of tapas, which I will explain in detail a little later.

If you have coca, that is, Catalan pita, do not leave the table without trying it.

Escalivada is served in almost every fish and meat restaurant in the city, as it is a complement. But if you want to eat the best, you can change your route to Sesamo (Sant Antoni Abat, 52, 08001) or Taverna El Glop (Carrer de Sant Lluís, 24, 08012).


Tapas
Tapas, according to many travelers, “What to eat in Barcelona?” It is one of the most ideal answers that can be given to the question.

Tapas, which is really one of the most logical options to suppress hunger, is served hot or cold depending on its content.

You can be sure that you can easily find a version suitable for your taste in these wonderful appetizers, whose name is derived from the verb "Tapar", which means "to cover" in our language. Because some types of tapas consist of fried vegetables and seafood or a mix of cold cuts of vegetables and pickles. Some are served in the form of a sauce that can be consumed with chips.

Catalans usually wait after work to eat tapas. Considering that local people have a habit of eating dinner as late as 9:00 pm, this course of action does not seem unreasonable at all.

If you want to suppress your deficit in an easy way while touring the attraction points in the city, you can choose the following places:
- El Puestu: C. de Ribes, 39, 08013
- Bormuth: Plaça Comercial, 1, 08003


Pa Amb Tomaquet
As I mentioned in the previous section, you can find the opportunity to taste many types of tapas in the restaurants and bars of the city. But if you want to use your right of choice in favor of the options that are the symbols of the local cuisine, you can give priority to Pa Amb Tomaquet.
br/> Pa Amb Tomaquet, which is among the recipes expressing the identity of Catalan cuisine, or as it is called in our language, tomato bread draws attention with its simplicity in its preparation. Because you can prepare this delicacy at home in a few minutes.

For this, the tomato is first mashed and blended with olive oil and some salt. It is then spread on toasted bread in a thin layer.

Pa Amb Tomaquet is generally consumed as an appetizer, but it can easily find a place for itself on breakfast tables.

You can go to Paco Meralgo to taste this flavor. Address: Calle Muntaner 171, 08036
Alternatively, Cal Pep is also a good option. Address: Plaça de les Olles, 8, 08003


Crema Catalana
If you want to eat something sweet while on holiday in Barcelona, you can pamper yourself with a bowl of Crema Catalana.

The dessert, which has been a veteran of Spanish cookbooks since the 14th century, resembles Crème Brûlée in terms of both appearance and consistency. Of course, I have to mention that this recipe is much older than its French counterpart.

Whole milk is the main ingredient of Crema Catalana. Cinnamon and lemon zest are added for flavor. Of course, don't be surprised if you see different forms of this dessert in food and beverage venues in the city.

Granja M. Viader is one of the most ideal places to eat Crema Catalana in Barcelona. Address: Carrer d'en Xuclà, 4-6, 08001


Churros
Frankly, a bowl of Crema Catalana is mind-blowing. But for those who like pastries, Churros always means something different.

If my sentence above sounds too pretentious to you, I would like to point out that the dessert's fame went beyond Spain centuries ago and spread to the entire American continent.

This flavor, which resembles a ring dessert in form, is served without syrup. But thanks to the chocolate sauce served with it, there is no need for such a touch. However, this sauce can also be offered for consumption by pouring it directly on the freshly fried pastry in some patisseries.

If you intend to pamper yourself with a crispy portion of Churros, you can add the following two places to your list of places to visit in Barcelona.

Granja La Pallaresa: Carrer de Petritxol, 11, 08002
La Nena: Carrer de Ramón y Cajal, 36, 08012
